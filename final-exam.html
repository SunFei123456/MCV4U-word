Question 1

Explain when you need to use the chain rule.

Show Solution

You need to use the chain rule when differentiating a composite function — a function of the form f(g(x)), where one function is nested inside another.

In other words, whenever the function you are differentiating can be written as an "outer" function applied to an "inner" function, the chain rule is required.

Formula:

d/dx [f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) · g'(x)

Examples:

• y = (2x + 3)5 → outer: u5, inner: 2x + 3

• y = sin(x2) → outer: sin(u), inner: x2

• y = √(3x - 1) → outer: √u, inner: 3x - 1

Question 2

The graphs of a function and its derivative are shown. Label the graphs f and f', and write a short paragraph stating the criteria you used to make your selection.

Show Solution

Label:

• The parabola-shaped curve (opening upward, with a minimum at the origin) is f.

• The straight line passing through the origin is f'.

Criteria Used:

1. Zeros of f' correspond to critical points of f: The straight line crosses the x-axis at x = 0, and the parabola has its minimum (horizontal tangent) exactly at x = 0.

2. Sign of f' determines increase/decrease of f: The straight line is negative when x < 0 and positive when x > 0. The parabola decreases on (−∞, 0) and increases on (0, ∞), matching perfectly.

3. Degree relationship: If f is a quadratic (degree 2), then f' should be linear (degree 1). The parabola and straight line fit this relationship.

Question 3

Use the definition of the derivative to find d/dx(x − x2).

Show Solution

Definition: f'(x) = limh→0 [f(x + h) − f(x)] / h

Let f(x) = x − x2

Step 1: Find f(x + h)

f(x + h) = (x + h) − (x + h)2 = x + h − x2 − 2xh − h2

Step 2: Compute f(x + h) − f(x)

= [x + h − x2 − 2xh − h2] − [x − x2]

= h − 2xh − h2

Step 3: Divide by h

[f(x + h) − f(x)] / h = (h − 2xh − h2) / h = 1 − 2x − h

Step 4: Take the limit as h → 0

f'(x) = limh→0 (1 − 2x − h) = 1 − 2x

Question 5

Determine the slope of the tangent to the graph of y = (x2 + 3x − 2)(7 − 3x) at (1, 8).

Show Solution

Method: Expand first, then differentiate.

y = (x2 + 3x − 2)(7 − 3x)

= 7x2 − 3x3 + 21x − 9x2 − 14 + 6x

= −3x3 − 2x2 + 27x − 14

dy/dx = −9x2 − 4x + 27

At x = 1:

dy/dx = −9(1) − 4(1) + 27 = −9 − 4 + 27 = 14

Answer: The slope of the tangent at (1, 8) is 14.

Question 6

Show Solution

Question 7

Determine the equation of the tangent to y = (3x−2 − 2x3)5 at (1, 1).

Show Solution

Step 1: Verify the point

At x = 1: y = (3(1)−2 − 2(1)3)5 = (3 − 2)5 = 15 = 1 ✓

Step 2: Differentiate using chain rule

dy/dx = 5(3x−2 − 2x3)4 · (−6x−3 − 6x2)

Step 3: Evaluate at x = 1

dy/dx = 5(3 − 2)4 · (−6 − 6) = 5(1)4 · (−12) = 5 · (−12) = −60

Step 4: Equation of tangent

y − 1 = −60(x − 1)

y = −60x + 60 + 1

y = −60x + 61

Question 8

The amount of pollution in a certain lake is P(t) = (t1/4 + 3)3, where t is measured in years and P is measured in parts per million (ppm). At what rate is the amount of pollution changing after 16 years?

Show Solution

Step 1: Differentiate P(t) using chain rule

dP/dt = 3(t1/4 + 3)2 · 14t−3/4

Step 2: Evaluate at t = 16

t1/4 = 161/4 = 2

t−3/4 = 16−3/4 = 18

dP/dt = 3(2 + 3)2 · 14 · 18

= 3(25) · 132

= 7532 ppm/year

Answer: The rate of change after 16 years is 7532 ppm/year (or approximately 2.34 ppm/year).

Question 10

Determine the points on the curve y = x3 − x2 − x + 1 where the tangent is horizontal.

Show Solution

Step 1: Find dy/dx and set equal to 0

dy/dx = 3x2 − 2x − 1 = 0

Step 2: Factor

(3x + 1)(x − 1) = 0

x = −13 or x = 1

Step 3: Find corresponding y-values

At x = −13:

y = (−13)3 − (−13)2 − (−13) + 1

= −127 − 19 + 13 + 1 = −1 − 3 + 9 + 2727 = 3227

At x = 1:

y = 1 − 1 − 1 + 1 = 0

Answer: The points are (−13, 3227) and (1, 0).

Question 11

For what values of a and b will the parabola y = x2 + ax + b be tangent to the curve y = x3 at point (1, 1)?

Show Solution

Condition 1: The point (1, 1) lies on the parabola.

1 = (1)2 + a(1) + b

1 = 1 + a + b

a + b = 0  →  b = −a

Condition 2: The slopes are equal at x = 1.

For y = x3: dy/dx = 3x2 → at x = 1, slope = 3

For y = x2 + ax + b: dy/dx = 2x + a → at x = 1, slope = 2 + a

Set slopes equal:

2 + a = 3

a = 1

Then b = −1

Answer: a = 1 and b = −1
